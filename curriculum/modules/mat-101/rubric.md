# Rubric: precise claims, proofs, and evidence scope

## Rubric

The assessment is worth 100 points.

| Criterion | Points | Evidence required |
| --- | ---: | --- |
| Statements, quantifiers, truth cases, and counterexamples | 30 | Accurate domains, predicates, negations, implication forms, truth classifications, witnesses, and counterexamples |
| Four proof techniques | 40 | Complete direct, contrapositive, contradiction, and induction arguments with justified steps and exact scope |
| Proof-versus-testing boundary | 20 | Reproducible finite checks, correct counterexample interpretation, separate assumption/scope inventories, and honest limits |
| Knowledge, notation, and clarity | 10 | Correct knowledge answers, defined symbols, readable reasoning, and traceable evidence |
| **Total** | **100** |  |

## Critical criteria

All three critical criteria must pass:

1. **Precise statement control — MAT-101-01:** earn at least 24/30. Quantifier
   domains and order must be explicit; negations must swap quantifiers correctly;
   the implication table must identify only true-antecedent/false-consequent as
   false; and every false universal claim must have a valid counterexample.
   Treating a converse as equivalent without proof fails this criterion.
2. **Proof construction — MAT-101-02:** earn at least 32/40 and at least 7/10 on
   each of the four proof methods. The induction argument must include a valid
   base case, arbitrary induction hypothesis, justified step, and scoped
   conclusion. Examples, circular reasoning, or a named method without its
   required steps fail this criterion.
3. **Evidence boundary — MAT-101-03:** earn at least 16/20. Preserve exact finite
   ranges and execution evidence, use a discovered counterexample to refute only
   its matching universal claim, and keep proof assumptions separate from
   program/test assumptions. Calling finite sampling over integers a proof fails
   this criterion.

Use of unrecorded external computation, network services, or copied proof text
fails the affected critical criterion even if the numerical total would
otherwise pass.

## Scoring

- **Statements, quantifiers, truth cases, and counterexamples (30):** 10 for the
  five quantified predicates and domains; 8 for correct negations; 6 for
  converse/inverse/contrapositive and truth cases; and 6 for valid witnesses,
  counterexamples, and truth decisions.
- **Four proof techniques (40):** 10 each for the direct, contrapositive,
  contradiction, and induction arguments. Within each, award 2 for exact
  proposition/domain, 2 for assumptions and arbitrary variables, 4 for justified
  derivation, and 2 for the correctly scoped conclusion.
- **Proof-versus-testing boundary (20):** 5 for reproducible command evidence, 5
  for exact range/count/counterexample interpretation, 6 for separate assumption
  inventories, and 4 for at least three accurate limits of each evidence form.
- **Knowledge, notation, and clarity (10):** 6 for the five knowledge answers, 2
  for defined and consistent notation, and 2 for readable, traceable files.

Pass the module only with at least 80/100 and all critical criteria.

## Feedback and retry

Feedback identifies the first invalid logical step, its outcome ID, and whether
the defect is in translation, method, algebra, or evidence scope. Preserve the
original answer and annotate the correction so the reasoning change remains
visible.

For a retry, the assessor changes the predicates, proof targets, and finite
range. The learner must redo every affected translation or proof and produce a
fresh execution record. A corrected conclusion without repaired intermediate
steps does not satisfy a critical criterion. The 80/100 threshold and all
critical criteria apply again.
